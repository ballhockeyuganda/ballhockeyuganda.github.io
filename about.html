Our Mission

Our mission is to create a big National Hockey League like those in first World Countries and enable Uganda participate in World championship.

Our Vision

To make sure that hockey spreads throughout Uganda's community and make hockey accessible wether in remote or urban areas.

Our Values


Our values include Transparency, Equality, Respect, Shared Happiness and Persistence.

Our Background

Uganda Ball Hockey Association was initiated by a Canadian journalist Mr. Thomas Froese together with his wife a female doctor, Dr. Jean Chamberlain the former director of save the mothers Uganda. In the year 2005 that's when ball hockey was born in uganda and it was for the Canadians and American community only. Until we got pitched into it through our Ugandan friend Paul Mukhwana who was their worker.

Hockey was a new and strange game which we have never seen before and it attracted many of us to join the team. As years moved on in the year 2017, the hockey story nearly came to an end until one of the most active players by the names of Captain Amon Matthew asked to take the hockey program forward and he asked for some gears to be left behind for a start and 23 sticks were left on ground including two goalie sticks and with those sticks he taught hundreds of people including schools, community kids and youths.

In the year 2019 Mr. Thomas Froese came back with the family to check on how far the hockey program has gone and he found it interesting that he found new faces of adults, youths and kids and he played a couple of games with them. In the year 2021 a friend from Slovenia from the company for hockey sticks donated sticks to the Uganda ball hockey community and he sent it to Uganda, Captain Amon donated 9 sticks to the Kenyan hockey community and 4 balls to give them a boost in the growth of the sport. Our main aim is to spread hockey on the African continent.

Who is Captain Amon?

By Mr. Richard and Mrs. Catherine Ranger

Fifteen years ago in an East African country without an ice rink, a young boy watched men slashing around an ice rink at incredible speeds, chasing a black hard rubber puck with long sticks. No sport he had seen before looked like more fun. When someone handed him a hockey stick, and showed him a ball, that was all it took to enlist Amon Matthew Ssekaana in Uganda’s newly growing sport of ball hockey. Here in Uganda, there may still be only a couple of ice rinks, and the rivers and lakes never freeze. But if you seek a sport with speed and non-stop action, along with a community of friends to play with, then ball hockey is your sport. Just ask Amon Matthew. He’s likely to be the one handing you a stick.

Back when Amon Matthew was a boy in love with a new sport, the coaches were likely to be ex-pat Canadians, looking for a way to enjoy Canada’s national pastime thousands of miles and across an ocean and continent from home. The Canadians saw Ugandan kids standing on the sidelines, excited by what they were watching, and could not resist handing them some sticks and inviting the young Ugandans to play. The Canadians may have returned home, but Ugandan ball hockey is here to stay. Amon Matthew will tell those interested in learning ball hockey to get ready for a workout. “It’s a full time running game”, he says. “You don’t stop. There can be plenty of contact – and kids like it”. Inadvertent contact is not a reason to stop the action and to bring out a card. It’s a reason to get up and get back into the play.

There are plenty of skills to learn to challenge the new player. Amon Matthew says that new players quickly find themselves asking many questions, but that Uganda’s welcoming ball hockey community is more than ready to share what they have learned. Like how a hockey stick can be a tool that works in three dimensions – to hit, propel, guard and protect, and to pass a ball to a teammate. The movement of the game can reverse itself in an instant, so ball hockey commands and rewards attention to the ball in front of you and the flow of play all around you. Amon Matthew describes ball hockey as one of the best exercises you can have. And, as among Uganda’s newer sports, ball hockey invites you into a community of new friends to share it with. There can be family games, games among skilled adult players, and youth games. There is a ball hockey opportunity out there for everyone. There may not be much ice here on the Equator in Uganda – but there can be plenty of non-stop action. Just be on the lookout for wherever ball hockey is played!
